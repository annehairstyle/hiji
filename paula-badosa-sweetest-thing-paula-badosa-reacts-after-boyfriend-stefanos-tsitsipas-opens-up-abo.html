Paula Badosa: &quot;Sweetest thing&quot; - Paula Badosa reacts after boyfriend Stefanos Tsitsipas opens up abo

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Monday, June 3, 2024

Former World No. 2 Paula Badosa recently reacted to her boyfriend and fellow tennis star Stefanos Tsitsipas’s heartwarming statement about his ‘Holy Trinity’.

The two tennis players went public with their relationship shortly after this year's Italian Open in Rome. Since then, the two have frequently shared glimpses of their journey together on their social media accounts.

Stefanos Tsitsipas, in a recent interview ahead of the Rolex Paris Masters, talked about his 'Holy Trinity' when asked about the things he is in love with.

“What are you in love with?” asked the reporter.

In reply, Tsitsipas named tennis, his girlfriend, and his family as the three things he was in love with, calling them his ‘holy trinity.'

“I’m in love with tennis, my girlfriend, and my family, these three things that I call the holy trinity,” he said.

A fan page of the couple was quick to share the snippet from the interview. Paula Badosa noticed this too and quickly posted a comment.

“The sweetest thing,” the Spanish tennis player wrote.

The 25-year-old Greek tennis star was recently defeated by Daniil Medvedev 6-4 7-6 (8-6) in the Vienna Open semifinals. He would now be looking to make a deep run at the Paris Masters.

Paula Badosa, on the other hand, is preparing to return to action in 2024, after suffering from recurring back problems this year. She decided to end her season ahead of the 2023 US Open citing injury.

“I’m going through the best time of my life” - Stefanos Tsitsipas while talking about girlfriend Paula Badosa

Stefanos Tsitsipas recently talked about the significance of having Paula Badosa by his side, while claiming that he was having the best time of his life.

During a press conference at the 2023 European Open in Antwerp, the 25-year-old talked at length about Badosa’s influence in his life.

"My meeting with Paula [Badosa] gave me another vision of life in general," he said. "Before, it was always tennis, tennis, tennis, that was all that mattered, 24 hours a day, seven days a week. And during that part of my young career, I didn’t spend enough time with my family."

He went on to talk about how he perceives personal connections.

"Maybe for some, these connections aren’t so important, but for me they are crucial," he said. "These are elements of my life that also require time. Not really energy, because they’re just part of me. So to have Paula by my side now is great. I’m going through the best time of my life. I have the right people around me, I have no doubts with that."
