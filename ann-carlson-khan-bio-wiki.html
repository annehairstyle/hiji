Ann Carlson Khan: Husband and Children!

Ann is married to Shahid Khan. Her husband is a Pakistani American billionaire businessman.

He is the owner of Flex-N-Gate, he also owns Jacksonville Jaguars of the NFL, Fulham F.C of the English premier league, and a lead investor in professional wrestling promotion All Elite Wrestling.

Ann and Shahid married on January 28, 1981, in Clark, Nevada, US.

How many children does Ann Carlson Khan have?

They have two children, their son Tony Khan and their daughter Shanna Khan.

Who is Ann Carlson Khan?

Ann Carlson Khan is an American social worker. She is also a dancer, choreographer, and performance artist.

She serves as the president of the charity initiative, the Khan Foundation.

Ann Carlson Khan- Birth, Age, Ethnicity, Education

Ann Carlson Khan was born on October 21, 1954, in Park Ridge, Illinois, US. She belongs to Scandinavian heritage.

As of 2023, Ann is 68 years old. The names of her father and mother are unable to track.

She completed her education at the University of Illinois, Champaign, Illinois, US.

In 1976, she graduated magna cum laude with a BFA in Modern Dance from the University of Utah, Utah, US. In 1983, she earned her degree in Dance from the University of Arizona.

Is Ann Carlson Khan active on social media?

Professional Life, Career

She started as a professional dancer. She has performed with Territory Dance Theater in Tucson, Arizona, US. Ann also performed with Susan Rethorst and Meredith Monk as well. In the 1990s, she ended her career as a dancer.

Today Ann is the President of the Khan Foundation.

Philanthropy

Ann and her husband, Shahid Khan are actively involved with several charities and fundraisers in Illinois and Jacksonville.

They started contributing through Jacksonville Jaguars’ Foundation focused on the betterment of the community.

They provide children and family programs, fitness programs, military and veterans programs, women’s health issues, and neighborhood revitalization. The foundation gives away over 11k game tickets for each season.

In 2016, Ann and her husband donated $1 million US to the establishment of the Baptist MD Anderson Cancer Center in Jacksonville, Florida, US. They also raised $2.25 million US for the treatment of breast cancer.

They have supported the Jacksonville Veterans Resource and Reintegration Center, North Florida Boy Scouts, Ronald McDonald House of Jacksonville, City Year of Jacksonville, Edward Waters College, YMCA of Florida’s First Coast, the American Cancer Society’s Hope Lodge of Jacksonville, the Yeardley Lone/One Love Foundation, and the Jacksonville Humane Society.

This couple has also donated to the University of Illinois’ Krannert Center of the Performing Arts, the College of Applied Health Sciences, the College of Business, and the library.

Their donation to the College of Health Sciences contributed to the funding of Five Khan Professorships as well as the Khan Annex.
