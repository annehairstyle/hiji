Early Life

Sheldon Jerome Young was born on May 26, 1991, between Compton and Lynwood in southern Los Angeles. His great-grandparents raised him. He spent most of his days at church, playing the drums for the choir. 

Channel moved away from home to attend Oral Roberts University, a private Christian university in Tulsa, Oklahoma.

He began playing the drums at the age of ten and went on to study music technology, composition, and theory at Oklahoma’s Oral Roberts University. Tres returned to Los Angeles after graduating college and began focusing on his music career.

Career

Tres began his career as a DJ and producer, and his debut EP, Channel Tres, was published in 2018. The EP received positive reviews, and Tres was named one of Pitchfork’s “Best New Artists of 2018.”

 Tres has toured widely in the United States and Europe, working with artists such as Tyler, the Creator, JPEGMAFIA, and Mura Masa.

 In 2020, he released his debut album, Black Moses. The album was a critical and financial success, earning it a Grammy nomination for Best Dance/Electronica Album.

Tres’ distinct sound combines house music, disco, funk, rap, and hip-hop elements. His music is frequently described as “gumbo,” a mash-up of many elements that would not normally be combined. Tres is a genre-bending artist who is quick to try out new sounds.
