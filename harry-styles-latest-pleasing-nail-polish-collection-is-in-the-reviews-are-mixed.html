Harry Styles' Latest Pleasing Nail Polish Collection Is In &amp; The Reviews Are Mixed

Posted by Billy Koelling on Sunday, June 9, 2024

Would you look at that. Turns out Pleasing's latest nail polish drop is perfectly aligned with Harry Styles's tour dates. Harry knew what he was doing, because this mesmerising collection of four pastel power nail polish shades is not only perfectly suited to an epic spring x summer time mani, but also to go with your Harry Styles concert outfit. That's if you managed to bag tickets, of course.

“Drawing inspiration from the wonders of nature and the remarkable creatures that contribute to the intricate ecosystem of pollination, each shade in the Pleasing The Pollinators Set is carefully crafted to embody the beauty and allure found in the great outdoors,” says the brand.

The Bell Boy Blue hue is a punchy blue opaque gloss formula that commands attention, and really makes a statement. You could also opt for Pond Goo, which is more of a muted green that really exudes tranquility, sophistication and outdoors joy.

For a touch of elegance, try the Bride’s Buttercream shade, which is an elevated glossy and opaque cream (basically off-white) that adds a refined and polished look to your nails. Then there's Peachy Cheeks, a vibrant poppy peach that radiates warmth and playfulness in just a few swipes.

The beauty of The Pollinators Set lies not only in the adorable Pleasing bubble top lids, or the dopamine-inducing colours, but also in its versatility. Each shade can be worn alone to create a bold and captivating look, mixed for epic nail art or even or layered over other colours to achieve personalised, iridescent and marbly effects. Whether you prefer a subtle accent or a loud-mani transformation, this set is here to offer you endless possibilities.

As the samples dropped in the GLAMOUR office, everyone wanted to give them a try. Here are five GLAMOUR's staffers honest opinions on each shade of the collection.

The Product:

Pleasing The Pollinators Set£72 at Pleasing

The Reviews:

Georgia, GLAMOUR's Commerce Writer

Shade: Bride’s Buttercream

I've become a gel girl ever since I invested in my own at-home kit, but every once in a while a new nail polish launch will peak my interest. Pleasing's latest line-up did exactly that, so, having already tried their first hues, I had to give them another whirl.

The Bride’s Buttercream shade I tested was all I wanted it to be and more. Pigmented, smooth, and super shiny. It's the epitome of summer in a bottle. It lasted just as well as I remember the first batch did, if not longer, so I'll be using it to create cute french tip looks all season long.

Rating: 9/10

Lian, GLAMOUR's Audience Development Manager

Shade: Pond Goo

This might well be the cutest nail polish packaging I've seen, with an equally adorable shade name to match: Pond Goo. Beyond being satisfying to look at, the bubble tops of these polishes serve an actual purpose, allowing for much easier grip for application than your average nail polish.

The first thing I noticed when using the product was that my first layer came up a little streaky. Having said that, I appreciate this is the case with a lot of polishes, especially those lighter shades. Opting for my usual two coats of colour I was able to create the opaque effect I was after, though I had expected more instantly defined colour on first swipe.

Having had it on for a few days, however, I can definitely attest to its staying power, with it yet to have chipped. Though the formula and consistency of the product wasn't especially standout to me, balanced with the great packaging and cute colour shades on offer in the range I'm a fan, and two coats is enough to get a decent opaque finish. This will be a great go-to for the warmer months when I'm after a smooth salon finish without the monthly nail salon bill.

Rating: 7/10

Buy It Now

Sophie, GLAMOUR's European Commerce Editor

Shade: Bell Boy Blue

I'm a gel nail mani addict through and through, so I didn't take the decision to ditch the usual manicure for a standard polish lightly. But anything for Harry Styles, right? I was more than pleasantly surprised – I used the shade Bell Boy Blue (which is dreamy) and only needed one coat to achieve the desired finish.

My main reason for loving it so much was how quickly it dried, though. Seriously: 5 minutes after application and I was totally ready to go about my normal day. And it stayed chip-free for a good couple of days.

Rating: 8/10

Luca, GLAMOUR's Social Media Manager

Shade: Peachy Cheeks

Summer is fast approaching (!) so when a new Pleasing nail polish dropped on my desk – a juicy, fresh-looking peach colour, aptly named Peachy Cheeks – I was excited. I've been giving my nails a rest from gel and acrylics recently so have been missing a pop of colour on my nails. I attempted a colourful French tip, applying the polish in tiny strokes along the top of my nail, something made easier by the flatter shape of the bristles on the brush.

It took a couple of coats to get a punchy colour pay-off, but I was feeling the end result. I was, however, quite disappointed to notice that one of my nails chipped partly off merely a few hours later. I'd be willing to give it another chance with a top coat but I wish it didn't need that to ensure staying power, especially at the high price point.

Rating: 6/10

Fiona, GLAMOUR's Acting Associate Beauty Director

Shade: Bell Boy Blue

I was immediately drawn to this cornflower blue colour as I love a pop of something bright on my nails in summer. My first impression was how creamy the formula is. There's no danger of a watery finish here. Next, is how evenly the colour went on.

The brush is quite beefy so it covers a large portion of your nail in one swipe – after two you're ready to go. The shine is also off the scale – it literally mimics that lust-worthy shellac finish. Best of all, the polish dries within a few minutes, which is perfect for someone like me who is time poor and loves a speedy beauty solution.

Rating: 8/10

Pleasing The Pollinators Set£72 at Pleasing
