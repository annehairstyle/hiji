Manti Te'o net worth: the fortune of the LB and star of 'Untold: The Girlfriend Who Didn't Exist'

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Monday, June 10, 2024

Manti Malietau Louis Te'o is an NFL Free agent who played for the New Orleans Saints, Chargers, and the Chicago Bears.

Manti Te'o played college at Notre Dame. He played 12 games of his freshman season and recorded 63 tackles.

In his first year at Notre Dame, Manti Te'o won the Freshman All-American honors.

He left Notre Dame with 437 total tackles and several awards such as the Maxwell Award, the Chuck Bednarik Award, the Butkus Award, the Lombardi Award, and the Walter Camp Award.

However, he is not famous for his NFL career, Manti had a catfishing situation that caused him the first-round pick into the NFL.

Manti played professional football as expected. Nevertheless, he lost millions in his contract for the catfishing event.

Manti Te'o catfishing documentary

Netflix released a documentary about Manti's catfishing situation called 'Untold: The Girlfriend Who Didn't Exist'.

When someone or something, in this case, Netflix, exposes an embarrassing situation, you expect people to make fun of or retaliate against you.

However, public opinion praised Manti Te'o for his courage and attitude after the catfishing situation.

Career Earnings

According to Celebrity Net Worth, during his NFL career, Manti Te'o earned around $10.4 million.

During the 2018 season with the New Orleans Saints, Manti Te'o earned $1.7 million, the highest wage he made in the National Football League.

Manti lost money due to his catfishing scandal. He was a Heisman finalist, and NFL analysts projected Manti as a first-round and top-half pick of the 2013 NFL draft.

The Charges drafted Manti as the 38th overall pick with a 4-year contract worth $5.1 million. He lost half of his value due to his catfish scandal.
