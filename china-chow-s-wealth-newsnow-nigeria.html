Discovering China Chow’s net worth: $10 Million

About China Chow

Born on April 15, 1974, China Chow is a prominent English actress and model known for her appearances in publications such as Vogue and Maxim, as well as for her role in the film “The Big Hit”.

As of mid-2017, authoritative sources estimated Chow’s net worth at $10 million, earned through her successful career as an actress and model which commenced in the early 1990s.

Net Worth: $10 Million

Chow was born in London, England to Michael Chow, a renowned restaurateur and the creator of Mr. Chow restaurant, and Tina Chow, a late model and designer. Her father is Chinese while her mother has German and Japanese ancestry. She also has a brother named Maximillian. Chow is the first college graduate in her family, holding a degree in Psychology from Scripps College in California.

Her professional journey took off when she was scouted by Roger Museenden, leading to modeling opportunities with prominent names like Tommy Hilfiger and Calvin Klein, and with Japanese beauty brand Shiseido. Her modeling years not only established her career but also contributed to her net worth.

Chow’s modeling success led to features in various fashion publications. In 1996, she was recognized in Harper’s Bazaar as one of their “It” girls for the year, and was included in Vogue’s “The Next Best-Dressed List”. Her striking appearance also landed her a spot in Maxim magazine’s “Girls of Maxim” gallery in 2000, and she was listed in the magazine’s Hot 100 Women of 2000 and 2001.

Besides excelling as a model, Chow delved into acting. Her debut came in 1998 with a role in the movie “The Big Hit” alongside Mark Wahlberg. She subsequently appeared in films such as “Head Over Heels”, “Spun”, and “Frankenfish”. Her achievements in the realm of cinema furthered her career and enriched her wealth.

In the late 2000s, Chow transitioned to TV, featuring in USA Network’s “Burn Notice” in 2007, and serving as the host and judge of the reality competition show “Work of Art: The Next Great Artist” in 2010. She also provided her voice for the video game “Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas” as Katie Zhan. These varied endeavors also contributed to augmenting her net worth.

Regarding her personal life, Chow is single, and has been in notable relationships with actor Mark Wahlberg and comedian Steve Coogan.

In her family life, Chow’s mother succumbed to AIDS in 1992.
