Beverly D’Angelo’s Recognition and Awards

Golden Globe Nomination for “Coal Miner’s Daughter”

Beverly D’Angelo has been recognized for her outstanding performances throughout her career, receiving nominations for several prestigious awards. One of her most notable achievements is her Golden Globe nomination for Best Supporting Actress in the biographical musical drama film “Coal Miner’s Daughter” in 1981. D’Angelo portrayed Patsy Cline, a country music icon, in the film, showcasing her versatility and talent as an actress.

“I was honored to be recognized for my role in ‘Coal Miner’s Daughter.’ It was a challenging and rewarding experience, and I am grateful for the nomination.” – Beverly D’Angelo

Emmy Nomination for “A Streetcar Named Desire”

In addition to her Golden Globe nomination, D’Angelo also received an Emmy nomination for her performance in the television adaptation of Tennessee Williams’ play “A Streetcar Named Desire” in 1984. She portrayed Stella Kowalski, a complex character whose portrayal earned D’Angelo critical acclaim and recognition from the Emmy Awards.

“Being nominated for an Emmy for my role in ‘A Streetcar Named Desire’ was a proud moment in my career. It was a privilege to bring Stella Kowalski to life on screen.” – Beverly D’Angelo

Country Music Association Award for Album of the Year

Outside of her acting accolades, D’Angelo received a Country Music Association (CMA) award for Album of the Year in 2020. The award recognized her contribution as a featured artist on the soundtrack album for the film “Patsy & Loretta,” in which she portrayed Patsy Cline once again. The album showcased D’Angelo’s musical talent and her ability to capture the essence of Patsy Cline’s iconic voice.
