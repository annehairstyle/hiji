The Jungle Book Set to Return Next Year

Posted by Billy Koelling on Monday, June 24, 2024

As children, most of us have grown up watching the happy-go-lucky Baloo living a peaceful life in the jungle with just his ‘Bare necessities of life’; or the royal black panther Bagheera with a soft heart for his man-cub friend Mowgli in the Jungle Book. The great news is that The Jungle Book is back in another movie adaptation. So, this time the audience would get a glimpse of all their favourite characters in  high definition quality. The trailer of this much awaited movie has been released and is gaining rapid popularity amongst the audience. Here is a first glimpse of the movie.

[youtube https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5mkm22yO-bs]

The Jungle Book is set to release next year  in April 2016. This movie will portray an amazing array of special effects and 3-D which has never been seen before. Apart from being a well-known classic which is being voiced by legendary actors,  The Jungle Book’s image capture quality and audio resonance is one of the best reasons why one should treat themselves to this movie.

This movie is directed by Jon Favreau who is the well- known for  Iron Man and Avengers. The movie has been shot in two different ways. It includes live actors as well as animated animals with voice-overs. Neel Sethi has played the role of the legendary man cub Mowgli; Sir Ben Kingsley has lent his voice to the humane yet strict panther Bagheera and Bill Murray as the overtly funny Baloo. Other actors include Idris Elba (Shere Khan), Lupita Nyong’o (Mother Wolf) and Scarlett Johansson (Kaa, the snake). Comic relief is brought by the majestic Colonel Haathi and King Louise (King of Monkeys). Needless to say, the storyline follows Mowgli’s adventures in the jungle as he starts an eventful journey to the man – village to save himself from being devoured by the ferocious Shere Khan.

The Jungle Book has for decades overwhelmed the children and thus, this movie has really high expectations from the audience when it releases next year.
