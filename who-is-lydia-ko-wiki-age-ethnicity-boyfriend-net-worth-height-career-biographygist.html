Lydia Ko Biography

Lydia was born in Seoul, South Korea, relocated to New Zealand with her family as an infant, and at the age of 12 became a citizen of that country. Prior to joining the professional golf tour, she attended Mairangi Bay Primary and Pinehurst School in Albany, New Zealand, where she also completed correspondence courses.

Her mother introduced her to the Pupuke Golf Club on Auckland’s North Shore when she was five years old, where she met her first coach, Guy Wilson, who trained her until 2013. When she was seven years old, she gained notoriety for participating in the New Zealand national amateur championships.

Career

Ko won the Samsung Women’s NSW Open on the ALPG Tour on January 29, 2012, making history as the youngest golfer to ever claim a professional golf championship. In 2015, she captured five major titles. At the Coates Golf Championship on February 2, 2015, Ko passed Inbee Park to become the top-ranked female professional golfer. For the third time in four years, she won the ISPS Handa New Zealand Open in 2016. She donated her winnings to aid those impacted by the significant earthquake that occurred only 11 minutes before the game was set to end.

Lydia competed in 26 competitions in 2017, but she was unable to claim any victories. Her global rating dropped to ninth place despite being in the top 10 ten times that year. She did not succeed in winning a major championship tournament the following year either, and as a result, her global ranking fell to the fourteenth spot. She split with her longtime coach David Leadbetter three years prior to 2019, and in 2019 she only won one golf tournament, no major titles, and fell to her lowest career position of 39th.
