Chris Lynn reveals epic Doug Bollinger sledge after having testicle removed

Posted by Billy Koelling on Friday, June 7, 2024

IT’S not a well-known fact, but Big Bash superstar Chris Lynn once fell victim to one of cricket’s cruellest injuries.

It’s a common theme at a cricket ground to laugh at a batsman hunching over after a particularly brutal blow to the groin, but this time was a little too serious for the young man to simply walk off.

Nearly four years ago in a Shield game, Lynn was struck in the lower region by Test paceman Doug Bollinger. What followed was every man’s nightmare.

“He was bowling and I tried to hit him out of the stadium,” Lynn told news.com.au. “I inside-edged one up into my box and ruptured my testicle.”

Lynn’s excruciating injury forced him to hospital to reduce swelling, but doctors eventually deemed the blow too severe to cure and made the final, chilling call to remove his testicle.

Now, years later, Lynn is able to laugh off the incident, recalling a brutal sledge delivered by Bollinger in the very next game they played against each other: “Dougie Bollinger was at mid-off and yelling out ‘come on lads! It’s only going to take one ball here!’”

“I thought it was quite smart from Doug,” Lynn added. “It’s all good now, though. That’s the main thing.”

Lynn’s incredible display in the Big Bash earned him Weet-bix’s “King of the Six” award for the second year in a row, an outstanding achievement considering he missed numerous games with a nagging shoulder injury.

“It’s a nice little achievement, but the disappointing thing is I’ve missed half the games,” he said. “You never know how many (sixes) I could have got, but it is what it is.”

When it comes to clearing the rope, the powerful Queenslander believes simplicity is key.

“I just have a whack in the nets,” he said. “I try and keep everything so simple. (It’s just) see ball, hit ball.

“I actually find it easier sometimes to go over the top instead of hitting a four purely so I don’t have to direct it through fielders. I know if I hit the ball clean over the top it’ll go for six, that comes through sheer practice.”

The rise of the “Lynnsanity” trend on Twitter turned the 26-year-old into a huge talking point, but he doesn’t let himself get a big head about all the stardom.

“I just do my business out in the middle,” Lynn said.

“It is a good feeling because you do get recognised, but at the end of the day it’s all about winning, that’s what really matters.”

Lynn broke into the international scene after being selected for Australia’s ODI series against Pakistan, but his stint in the green and gold was cut short with yet another shoulder problem forcing him to the sideline.

It’s obvious Lynn has made his fame from his stellar T20 form, but his first-class average of 44.39 from 40 matches puts him in the upper echelon of state batsmen vying for a position in Australia’s Test side. Having said that, Lynn thinks a Baggy Green is still a fair way off.

“I haven’t really played much red ball cricket,” he said.

“With the Big Bash being so big and being telecasted around the world you get recognised as a white ball specialist.”

“You never know what’s around the corner. My body just hasn’t been the greatest over the past few years, so hopefully we can put an end to that.

“I think I’m well off at the moment (even though) I haven’t played much cricket. It’s every kid’s dream to wear the Baggy Green, but I also think there’s so much opportunity with franchise cricket going on at the moment. There are financial opportunities if you want to play T20 cricket and it’s slightly easier on your body as well.”
