With his tactical understanding and ability to develop young talent, Rodgers has earned a reputation as one of the most successful managers in the sport.

Early Life & Education

Brendan Rodgers, currently 50, was born on January 26, 1973, in Carnlough, Northern Ireland. Growing up in a seaside village, he developed a love for football from an early age.

Rodgers was raised in a Catholic family, with his father, Malachy Rodgers, working as a painter and decorator and his Kristina, mother, volunteering for the Irish charity Trócaire.

He is the eldest of five boys, and his younger brother, Malachy, has also pursued a career in the music industry.

Growing up, Rodgers was an avid supporter of Sheffield Wednesday and Celtic.

Rodgers attended St John’s Catholic Primary School in Carnlough before moving to St Patrick’s College, Ballymena. At 16, he left school to pursue his football career.

Rodgers was keenly interested in the sport and represented Northern Ireland at schoolboy level, even playing against Brazil in 1988.

His talent as a footballer led him to sign for Ballymena United, his local team, where he started his senior career.

However, his playing career was cut short due to a genetic knee condition, forcing him to retire at 20.

Personal Life

Brendan Rodgers has had a romantic relationship with Charlotte Searle. The couple engaged in February 2016 and tied the knot in June 2017.

Together, they have two children: a son named Anton and a daughter named Mischa. Rodgers has been open about his admiration for his family and the support they have provided him throughout his career.

Career

Following his retirement from playing, Brendan Rodgers transitioned into coaching and management. He started his coaching career at Reading, serving as the academy director.

Rodgers gained valuable experience in Spain, studying various coaching methods, before being invited by José Mourinho to join Chelsea as their head youth coach in 2004.

He quickly rose through the ranks, eventually becoming the reserve team manager.

2008, Rodgers took on his first managerial role at Watford, followed by a brief stint at Reading in 2009. However, his tenure at Swansea City from 2010 to 2012 brought him widespread recognition.

Rodgers led Swansea to promotion to the Premier League, becoming the first Welsh team to achieve this feat. He also guided the team to an impressive 11th-place finish in their debut season in the top flight.

Rodgers’ success at Swansea caught the attention of Liverpool, who appointed him as their manager in 2012. While at Liverpool, he came close to winning the Premier League title in the 2013-2014 season, ultimately finishing as runners-up.

After leaving Liverpool in 2015, Rodgers took charge of Celtic in 2016 and achieved unparalleled success. Under his guidance, Celtic won numerous domestic trophies, including several Scottish Premiership titles and Scottish Cups.

In February 2019, Brendan Rodgers joined Leicester City as their manager. He led the team to victory in the 2021 FA Cup, their first-ever triumph.

Despite facing challenges during the 2022-2023 season, Rodgers left a lasting impact on Leicester City before departing in April 2023.

Net Worth
