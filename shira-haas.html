Shira Haas

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Friday, June 7, 2024

Shira Haas is an Israeli actress who is best known for her role in the film Noble Savage, for which she won the Israeli Academy Award. Unorthodox, a Netflix miniseries, has also helped her achieve international fame. Asia, The Zookeeper’s Wife, and Unorthodox were the TV shows and films that catapulted her to fame.

Shira Haas was born in the Israeli city of Tel Aviv in 1995. Her zodiac sign is Taurus, and her birthday is on the 11th of May every year. She is an American citizen with a Jewish ancestry.

Salary, Net Worth, and Income:

Shira started her acting career in 2013 when she played Ruchami Weiss in the Israeli drama series Shtistel. Princess, an Israeli drama film, featured her. The Hollywood Reporter and The New York Times also praised her for her outstanding lead performance.

In 2014, she won the Best Actress Award at the Jerusalem Film Festival, followed by the Best Actress Award at the Peace & Love Film Festival in 2015.

Shira eventually made her Hollywood debut in 2015 with the film A Tale of Love and Darkness. She has also appeared in television shows such as Harem, HaMenatzeah, Hazoref, and Ikaron HaHachlafa.

Shira was nominated for an Ophir Award in 2017 for Best Supporting Actress for the film Foxtrot. Then, in 2018, she received the Ophir Award for the Drama film Noble Savage. In the same year, she starred as Leah in the biblical drama film Mary Magdalene.

Shira has also appeared in TV shows and films such as Asia, Esau, Unorthodox, Noble Savage, and several others.

Eran Haas and Ariela Wrubell Haas are Shira’s parents. Her father is of Ashkenazi Jewish ancestry, while her mother is of Czech ancestry. Dotan Haas, her younger sister, is her sibling.
