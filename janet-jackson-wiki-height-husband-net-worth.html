Bio of Janet Jackson

Janet Damita Jo Jackson was born in Gary, Indiana, on May 16, 1966. Her parents are Joe Jackson, a skilled manager and one of the Jackson family’s founders of famous individuals, and Katherine Jackson.

The entire Jackson family is well-known and has given the world a variety of abilities. Her brothers are Brandon Jackson, who passed away a day after his birth, Jermaine Jackson, Randy Jackson, Tito Jackson, Jackie Jackson, Marlon Jackson, and Jackie Jackson. The two sisters of Janet Jackson are La Toya Jackson and Rebbie Jackson. She went to Lanai Road Elementary and Portola Middle Schools.

Career

Janet Jackson was raised in a musical household. Her father helped her sign a deal with A&M Records, which led to the 1982 publication of her self-titled debut album. “Dream Street,” Janet Jackson’s second studio album, was released two years later and performed well on the charts but did not debut at the top.

After the first two albums, Janet Jackson wanted to work independently and leave the Jackson family. In 1986, she collaborated with Jimmy Jam and Terry Lewis on her third album, “Control.” Janet made her debut on the Billboard 200 chart as well as the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums chart in the United States.

2015 saw the release of Janet Jackson’s 11th studio album, “Unbreakable,” which was well received. Numerous publications ranked it first, and it reached number one on numerous charts. She has additionally made appearances in several movies and TV shows, most notably in the drama “For Colored Girls.”
