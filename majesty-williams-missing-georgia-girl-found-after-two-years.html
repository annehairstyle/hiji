Majesty Williams Missing: Georgia Girl Found After Two Years

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Thursday, June 6, 2024

The news of Majesty Williams missing shook the nation, and her eventual safe return was a cause for celebration. 

Majesty was only five years old when she was abducted by her mother, Andrea McCord, on April 1, 2021. 

Her father, James Williams, reported the abduction to the Smyrna Police Department. 

However, it wasn’t until two years later that she was finally located in Mexico’s San Luis Potosi on July 17. 

The efforts of the Smyrna Police Department, NCMEC, and the US Marshal service, among others, helped to reunite Majesty and James Williams. 

This serves as a reminder to families experiencing difficulties to seek legal help, instead of resorting to illegal means. 

Let’s provide a detailed account of Majesty Williams’ abduction and her eventual reunion with her father.

Timeline of Majesty Williams’ abduction

James Williams and Andrea McCord were already estranged when McCord visited her daughter at James’ home in Smyrna, Georgia.

However, Andrea allegedly abducted Majesty Williams and stole James Williams’ car during this visit, before driving away from the scene. 

She later texted James Williams, stating that she had abandoned his car at a grocery store.

James Williams reported the abduction to the Smyrna Police Department. 

They also issued a felony arrest warrant for Andrea McCord, accusing her of Interstate Interference with Child Custody. 

However, the case was elevated to national attention since McCord was difficult to locate. 

The Missing Kids Organization featured Majesty Williams’ story on the TV show On Patrol: Live, hoping to generate public attention and lead to her location.

The Search for Majesty Williams missing

The National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) collaborated with the Smyrna Police Department and the US Marshals Service to track Andrea McCord and Majesty Williams. 

However, it was not easy since McCord had fled with the child to Mexico. 

Thus, the authorities shared the concerned video clips of Majesty Williams with NCMEC, which helped viewers connect with this sweet child. 

The show’s viewers wanted to help James Williams find his daughter, leading to a more active search for her.

Eventually, the authorities were able to locate Andrea McCord and Custodio Geurra in San Luis Potosi, after two years of searching. 

They were both arrested for their involvement in the abduction. 

The Smyrna Police Department expressed gratitude for their collaboration with NCMEC, which was instrumental in the search for Majesty Williams.

The reunion of James & Majesty Williams

On Sunday, July 16, after two years of being apart, James and Majesty Williams met again at the Hartfield-Jackson International Airport in Atlanta, Georgia. 

Following their reunion, James Williams expressed his gratitude to the authorities for their roles in the successful search. 

Majesty Williams is now safe at home with her father, who is overjoyed to have her back in his life.

The story of Majesty Williams proves that with perseverance,and determination, missing children can be found and reunited with their families.
