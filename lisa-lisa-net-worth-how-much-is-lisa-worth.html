Career as Lisa Lisa and Cult Jam

Lisa Lisa and Cult Jam emerged as a prominent freestyle music group in the early 1980s. The band featured Lisa Lisa as the lead singer, Alex “Spanador” Mosely as the guitarist/bassist, and Mike Hughes as the drummer and keyboardist.

They made their debut with the album “Lisa Lisa & Cult Jam With Full Force” in 1985. The album achieved platinum status and reached No. 16 on Billboard’s Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums chart. It featured the hit single “I Wonder If I Take You Home,” which not only topped Billboard’s Hot Dance/Disco chart but also earned a gold certification.

Building on their success, Lisa Lisa and Cult Jam continued to release albums that captivated audiences. “Spanish Fly,” released in 1987, showcased their versatility and cemented their position in the industry. The album included the popular track “Lost In Emotion.” In 1989, they released “Straight To The Sky,” further expanding their musical repertoire. The album’s hit single “Head To Toe” also achieved gold status.
