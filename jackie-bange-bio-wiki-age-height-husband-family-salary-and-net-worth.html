    Jackie Bange WGN-TV

    Jackie started her broadcasting career as a reporter and noon anchor for the CBS affiliate WPEC-TV in her native place. She witched to NBC‘s WMAQ-TV in Chicago in 1990. Jackie joined WGN-TV’s News team in August of 1993 as a general assignment reporter. She presents WGN Morning News from January 1995 till October 1995.

    Bange transferred to anchor WGN News at Nine on weekend evenings alongside Robert Jordan in 1995. She is still working for News at Nine. Bange serves on the board of Michael Matters, an organization created to help those fighting brain cancer. She is involved in the fundraising efforts for A Safe Place, a domestic violence prevention organization in Lake County.

    Other awards Jackie has won include the Best Feature Reporting Emmy in 1999 for her series on attaining the world record for the largest skydive formation. Bange also won the Peter Lisagor award in the year 2009 for Hard News and In-Depth Reporting by helping a widow “get justice” for her husband who was murdered by a suspected drunk driver.
