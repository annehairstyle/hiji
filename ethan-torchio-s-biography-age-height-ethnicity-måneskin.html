Early years and career

Ever since Ethan was young, he enjoyed playing the drums and would take lessons on how to do it. For a while, the band played at the Monteverde High School in Rome and would occasionally earn some extra money by playing in the streets. The four came up with their band's name when they signed up for the Pulse contest. Måneskin basically means 'moonlight' in Danish and is a reference to Victoria's Danish heritage.

In 2017, the band participated in the Italian version of the music show X-Factor, where they ended up in second place. After that, their popularity in Italy grew remarkably. After their success on X-Factor, they released two studio albums: Il Ballo Della Vita, which came out in 2018, and Teatro d'ira: Vol. I, which was released in 2021.

Besides that, the band has since topped numerous Italian and international charts. In March 2021, Ethan and the band won the Sanremo Music Festival with their song Zitti e Buoni, which loosely translates to 'shut up and behave'.

This victory earned them a spot at the Eurovision Song Contest in 2021. Eventually, in May of the same year, Thomas Raggi, Victoria De Angelis, Ethan Torchio, and Damiano David were announced winners of the Eurovision contest. They received a total of 524 points from both jury votes and televotes and gave Italy its third Eurovision victory after the country's triumphs in 1964 and 1990.

After Eurovision, Ethan and the band performed at various festivals participated in live concerts and recorded new music. Their single I Wanna Be Your Slave became internationally successful and topped multiple charts.
