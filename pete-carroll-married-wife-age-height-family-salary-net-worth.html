Pete Carroll Biography and Age

On September 15, 1951, the Seahawk’s director was born and named Peter Clay Carroll in San Francisco, California. He is of Irish and Croatian descent and was raised in California by his parents, Rita (née Ban) and James Edward Carroll.

For his education, Pete went to Redwood High, a school where he found it very difficult to play sports due to his physical structure, although he was very good at athletics when he was growing up. Nonetheless, he would go on to play football, basketball and baseball for which he was good enough to be honored as Athlete of the Year in 1969.

After graduating from high school, Pete went to Marin College before moving to Pacific University, but was unable to turn pro after graduation.

He started his career as a graduate assistant for the Pacific Tigers football team, then the Arkansas Razorbacks. After rising through the ranks, he became the head coach of the New York Jets in 1994 before joining the San Francisco 49ers as defensive coordinator in 1995. Two years later he was the head coach of the New England Patriots until 1999. Pete Carroll was named head coach of USC in 2001 and served there until 2009. In 2010 he moved to the Seattle Seahawks, where he remains.

Named the Home Depot Coach of the Year in 2003, Pete Carroll won the Super Bowl in 2013 and the NFC Championship in 2013 and 2014. More so, he is a two-time National Champion, Rose Bowl four times, Orange Bowl twice. and Pac-10 seven times.

He was inducted into the USC Athletic Hall of Fame in 2015, the Redwood High School Athletic Hall of Fame in 2009, and the Pacific Athletic Hall of Fame in 1995.
