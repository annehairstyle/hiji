Early Life & Biography

Johnson was born on July 14, 1975. His place of birth was  Enterprise, Alabama. However, he grew up in Montgomery, Alabama. Even during his childhood, he was influenced by country music acts such as Alabama and Alan Jackson. After he graduated from Jefferson Davis High School, Johnson joined Jacksonville State University. During his time at Jacksonville State University, Johnson was a member of the Marching Southerners.

Johnson never graduated from university, for he quitted after two years and served in the United States Marine Corps Reserve. He served in the Marine for eight years. While in Marines, he began playing country music in various bars in the Montgomery area. By 2000, Johnson left for Nashville, Tennessee, to pursue a career in country music. Johnson was well of connections, for he had already established links with some of the best-known country music artists and producers.

Career

By 2005, Johnson had signed a recording contract with BNA Records. His first single, The Dollar, was released that year. This song went on to peak at number 14 on the Billboard Hot Country Songs charts. However, he lost his record deal and later divorced his wife. After that, he started living a reclusive lifestyle in a friend’s house and also working on his songwriting at the same time. He wrote several songs for other artists. The songs made a hit to the top list of the best country music.

In 2008, Johnson released album, That Lonesome Song. This one was only available online.  The album caught the attention of Mercury Nashville Records. That’s why they signed him, giving him a record deal in 2008. In March that same year, he released the first single for the label, In Color, Its release was in March 2008. The song entered Top 40 in June 2008. The Lonesome Song was certified Gold by RIAA. In July of the following year, he released his fifth single dubbed My Way to You.

On September14, 2010, Johnson released another album, The Guitar Song. Following its release, The Guitar Song received overwhelming praise and universal acclaim from music critics. Johnson then released three singles from the album, including, Playing the Part, in summer 2010. In 2011, Johnson supported Kid Rock during Born Free Tour, being with Rock during most concerts.

Besides producing other acts and writing new material, Johnson released his fourth studio album titled, Living for a Song: A Tribute to Hank Cochran, on October 6, 2012. The albums released on vinyl was on September 25, 2012. Other formats were released on October 16, 2012. Upon a dispute with his label, Johnson founded his label, Big Gassed Records. His first release on the name was a Christmas-themed EP, The Christmas Song, including several covers and one new original song, which was released in November 2014.

In 2019, he played alongside Warren Haynes, Don Was, Lukas Nelson, together with Cyril Neville on life even performing The Last Waltz Tour, during The Band’s 1976 farewell show.

Awards & Achievements

Johnson earned three Grammy Award nominations during the 51st Grammy Awards: They included Best Country Song Best Country Album for That Lonesome Song and Best Male Country Vocal Performance thanks to the In Color.

The song won the Academy of Country Music’s 2009 award earning the title Song of the Year. That was the same case during the 2009 Country Music Association Awards. In 2010, Johnson received two more Grammy Award nominations for the 52nd Grammy Awards: Best Country Song and Best Male Country Vocal Performance, both for his 2009 single, High Cost of Living.
