Former bikie Dillon Mancuso rescued after allegedly being kidnapped in Sydney

Posted by Artie Phelan on Saturday, June 8, 2024

Ten people have been charged over their alleged involvement in the late night kidnapping of a former bikie.

Tactical police officers swooped on a burning factory in Sydney’s west just after 1pm yesterday after hours of negotiations broke down.

A musclebound former bikie, dressed only in his tight white underwear, was rescued from the building by police.

NSW Police allege the semi-naked man, identified as Dillon Mancuso, had been kidnapped from his Bringelly home, in Sydney’s southwest at 3am yesterday in an attempted $2 million ransom plot.

Police were told a group of men – one allegedly wielding a firearm – forced their way into the house and confronted Mancuso, 37, and his 61-year-old dad Joe.

Joe was allegedly assaulted by the group before Dillon was forced into a car outside.

Joe was treated by paramedics at the scene for minor injuries.

The group were tracked by detectives from the State Crime Command’s Robbery and Serious Crime Squad to an industrial warehouse on Marigold St, Revesby.

Footage taken by a witness showed heavily armed police from the Tactical Operations Unit (TOU) storming the smoking warehouse.

Mancuso, a former Lone Wolf bikie, was seen on camera, perched on the end of a police vehicle wearing his undies.

He was later taken to hospital with non-life threatening injuries to his head and face.

Fire and Rescue NSW attended to extinguish the warehouse blaze.

Detective Superintendent Andrew Koutsoufis said the alleged ransom plot had been linked to “organised crime and bikies”.

“The level of violence towards the alleged victim was quite severe and concerning. We believe it was completely targeted,” he said.

Eight males – aged between 16 and 37 – were arrested by tactical police.

A further three men – aged 30, 28 and 27 – were arrested after arriving to the property in a vehicle during the operation.

All 11 males were taken to Liverpool and Bankstown Police Stations.

Seven men – two aged 23 and the others aged 27, 26, 22, 19, and a 16-year-old boy – were charged with take/detain in company with intent to ransom occasion actual bodily harm, special aggravated break and enter with intent – armed with dangerous weapon, tamper with evidence with intent to mislead judicial tribunal, and participate criminal group contribute criminal activity.

They were all refused bail to appear at court today.

The two men aged 23 and the 27-year-old man will appear at Bankstown Local Court while the men aged 26, 22, and 19, will appear at Parramatta Local Court.

The 16-year-old boy will appear at Parramatta Children’s Court.

Three men – aged 27, 28, and 30 – were charged with principal in the second degree to take/detain in company with intent to ransom occasion actual bodily harm, and participate criminal group contribute criminal activity.

They were refused bail to appear at Parramatta Local Court today.

A 37-year-old man was taken to hospital under police guard for treatment.

Inquiries into the incident are continuing.

Mancuso, while an alleged victim in yesterday’s plot, is also due in court.

Last year he was charged by the Australian Federal Police for dealing with the proceeds of crime after more than $1 million cash and three luxury cars valued at more than $100,000 each were seized by officers.
