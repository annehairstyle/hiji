Biblical Meaning of The Hand Of God (Gods Hands)

Posted by Artie Phelan on Friday, June 14, 2024

In the Bible, the technique, of ‘Anthropomorphism’ has been used several times. The term is said to represent a deeper understanding of God almighty using human descriptors.

Literarily, our God does not possess hands and yet, his hands have been mentioned most often in the Bible. What is, therefore, the importance of the vitality of God as mentioned in the Holy Scriptures?

Bible Verses About God’s Hands

  • “Behold, I have engraved you on the palms of my hands; your walls are continually before me” (Isaiah 49:16).
  • “Fear not, for I am with you; be not dismayed, for I am your God; I will strengthen you, I will help you, I will uphold you with my righteous right hand” (Isaiah 41:10).
  • “My hand laid the foundation of the earth, and my right hand spread out the heavens; when I call to them, they stand forth together” (Isaiah 48:13).
  • You have a mighty arm; strong is your hand, high your right hand (Psalm 89:13).
  • “From now on the Son of Man shall be seated at the right hand of the power of God” (Luke 22:69).
  • Psalm 8:3 even declares the glory of “the work of your fingers.”

Can We Hold God’s Hands?

God’s hands are extended to us, humans as a gesture of peace. They shield us from any uncertain, certain, known and unknown forces that seek to destroy us. We can put our trust in his hands which can easily crush our enemies and all evildoers.

We can’t therefore, trust ourselves but can wholly trust God whose hands created and saved us just as Revelation 1:7 says “Behold, he is coming with the clouds, and every eye will see him, even those who pierced him”. 

What Does God’s Hand Represent?

According to the Bible, God’s hands fully represents divine approval, acceptance of His sacrifice and also the storms mentioned in the gospels. His van is referenced in the Ascension of Jesus Christ, at times as in the Drogo Sacramentary, going down and holding forth to Christ and pulling him up into the clouds during his ascension to hold Father in heaven.

What Is The Hand Of God In The Bible?

Biblically, the right hand of God also known as Dextera Domini in Latin refers to the omnipotence of Almighty God and as the motif of his magnificent motif in art.

The Bible talks about God’s arm in Isaiah 40:10–11, ESV saying “Behold, the Lord God comes with might, and his arm rules for him; behold, his reward is with him, and his recompense before him. He will tend his flock like a shepherd; he will gather the lambs in his arms; he will carry them in his bosom, and gently lead those that are with young”.
