Lia Mortensen Chicago and lia mortensen obituary

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Sunday, June 30, 2024

In 1996, Remy Bumppo Theatre Company Pioneers Chicago's Literary Theater Scene

In 1996, a new Chicago theater named Remy Bumppo Theatre Company made its entrance by declaring its intention to cast the city's best actors in the most literate works it could find. The company aimed to provide audiences with thought-provoking performances and engage them with intellectually stimulating plays. One of the early standout performances came from Lia Mortensen, who played the neurotic Ruth in Tom Stoppard's "Night and Day." Mortensen's presence and luminous performance signaled that Remy Bumppo had its act together and was ready to make a mark on the Chicago theater scene.

Lia Mortensen: A Life in Theater

Lia Mortensen, born on June 15, 1965, was raised in Evanston as the daughter of two prominent academics at Northwestern University. Her late father, Dale T. Mortensen, was awarded the Nobel Prize in economics in 2010. After graduating from Indiana University in 1987, Lia returned to the Chicago area to pursue her acting career. She quickly immersed herself in the vibrant theater scene, working predominantly in nonprofit theater productions. Lia's dedication to her craft and exceptional talent propelled her into leading roles during the booming years of Chicago theater in the 1990s and 2000s.

Highlighting Mortensen's Acting Prowess

Throughout her career, Lia Mortensen showcased her talent and versatility in a wide range of roles. Her portrayal of Sally Talley in Lanford Wilson's "Talley's Folly" at Northlight Theatre in 2002 captured the complexities of a character trying to find love by shedding emotional baggage. Another notable performance came in Steve Scott's 2007 Goodman Theatre production of "Rabbit Hole." Mortensen delivered a wrenching performance as a mother coping with the loss of a child and navigating a new life amidst unimaginable circumstances. Her ability to portray characters facing trauma with veiled emotions that gradually erupt with intensity added depth and authenticity to her portrayals.

Lia Mortensen's Standout Role: Grace in "Faith Healer"

Among Mortensen's repertoire of memorable performances, her favorite role was that of Grace in J.R. Sullivan's production of Brian Friel's "Faith Healer." This play, which had a successful run in the 1990s at both the Turnaround Theatre and Steppenwolf Theatre Company, resonated deeply with Mortensen. Her embodiment of Grace showcased her exceptional talent and captivated audiences. In 2012, Mortensen reprised her role in the remounted production of "Faith Healer." Despite personal challenges related to maintaining relationships within the context of an artistic career, Mortensen's commitment to her craft remained unwavering, leaving a lasting impact on those around her.

A Daring Journey: Mortensen's Exploration of Musical Theater

In 2016, Lia Mortensen stepped outside her comfort zone by taking on the role of Joanne in the Writers Theatre production of Stephen Sondheim's "Company." Though she may have seemed too young for the character, director William Brown reimagined Joanne as an empowered go-getter, unafraid to confront the challenges of wisdom and temptation. Mortensen's performance once again highlighted her willingness to take artistic risks and push her boundaries, captivating audiences with her portrayal of a multifaceted character.

Remembering Lia Mortensen: A Timeless Talent

Lia Mortensen's impact on fellow actors and the Chicago theater community cannot be overstated. She was known for elevating the performances of those around her, bringing out the best in her fellow actors. Her daughter Jesse Osborne, who witnessed her mother's brilliance both on and off the stage, expressed awe at Mortensen's ability to captivate audiences. Mortensen's zest, brilliance, and unwavering dedication to her craft will be remembered by all who had the privilege of witnessing her performances.

Survivors and Memorial Service

Lia Mortensen is survived by her mother, Beverly P. Mortensen, her daughters Jesse and Ozzy Osborne, and her partners Marisol Garcia and Lily Turner. Plans for a memorial service to honor her life and contributions to the theater community are currently pending.

Conclusion

Lia Mortensen's untimely passing leaves a void in the Chicago theater scene. Her luminous performances, deep character portrayals, and willingness to take artistic risks have solidified her legacy as one of the city's finest actors. Lia Mortensen's contribution to Remy Bumppo Theatre Company and her numerous acclaimed roles will forever be cherished and celebrated.
