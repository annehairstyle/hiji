Who is Tiffany Stratton's boyfriend? Know all about Ludwig Kaiser

Posted by Artie Phelan on Wednesday, June 26, 2024

Born on May 1, 1999, in the United States, Jessica Woynilko, professionally known as Tiffany Stratton, had a background in gymnastics before venturing into the world of professional wrestling. Graduating from St. Catherine University in 2019, her journey into wrestling began when her mother contacted Greg Gagne, a former wrestler known for his time in the American Wrestling Association (AWA). Gagne took her under his wing, and the journey to becoming Tiffany Stratton commenced.

Professional Wrestling Debut

Tiffany made her professional wrestling debut in December 2021 after intense training under Greg Gagne. Her initial appearance showcased a glamorous woman, setting the stage for what would become an impressive career.

WWE Career (2021–Present)

Joining WWE on August 30, 2021, Tiffany Stratton quickly made her mark. Her debut on the November 16, 2021 episode of 205 Live saw her victorious against Amari Miller. Adopting a heel persona, she embraced the role of a spoiled rich girl upon her NXT debut, defeating Fallon Henley on December 28, 2021.

Tiffany engaged in memorable feuds, notably with Sarray and Wendy Choo, showcasing her versatility and determination. Her victory at NXT Battleground in a tournament secured her the NXT Women’s Championship in May, marking a significant milestone in her career. Tiffany’s reign included a successful title defense against Thea Hail at Gold Rush, exhibiting her resilience.

Who is Tiffany Stratton’s boyfriend?

As some rumors suggest, Tiffany Stratton is presently dating fellow WWE RAW superstar Marcel Barthel, who is widely known by his ring name Ludwig Kaiser. Kaiser was born in Pinneberg, Schleswig-Holstein, West Germany on July 8, 1991. Ludwig is currently 32 years old. Ludwig Kaiser is a professional wrestler who works for The RAW brand. He wrestled in Germany under the ring name Axel Dieter Jr. before joining WWE. Ludwig is most known for his stint with Westside Xtreme Wrestling (wXw), where he held the World Tag Team Championship twice and the Unified World Wrestling Championship once.
