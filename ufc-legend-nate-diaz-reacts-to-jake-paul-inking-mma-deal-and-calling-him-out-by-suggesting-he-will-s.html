Posted by Artie Phelan on Friday, June 7, 2024

UFC legend Nate Diaz has reacted to Jake Paul signing an MMA deal by suggesting he will join an alternative fight promotion.

On Thursday, ‘The Problem Child’ revealed he has signed with the Professional Fighters League [PFL] – a rival MMA promotional company to Dana White’s UFC.

The 25-year-old is planning to take a boxing bout against Tommy Fury or UFC veteran Mike Perry before making his MMA debut later this year.

Paul proposed that Diaz be the man to welcome him to a new sport.

The former Disney actor wants them to meet in boxing first and then clash in the PFL ‘Super Fight’ division in MMA four to six months later.

“I would beat Nate Diaz up in a MMA fight,” Paul said.

“There’s nothing he could do. The biggest thing for me would be getting the kicks down. But I’m going to start now, moving forward with some extra time I have within this camp.

“I’m going to put in some extra time, rolling around doing some jujitsu and learning some kicks.”

In September, Diaz announced his intention to leave the MMA leader after fighting out his contract with a fourth-round submission win over Tony Ferguson at UFC 279.

Since then, he has officially entered free agency and is currently fielding offers ahead of his next fight which he has suggested will take place in the boxing ring.

After receiving a call out from Paul, Diaz took to social media to distance himself from the fight by suggesting he will sign for RIZIN by posting a picture of their logo.

The Japanese fight promotion has been a major part of the post-fight career of boxing legend Floyd Mayweather who has fought five times since retiring.

In 2018, ‘Money’ travelled to Saitama to take on kickboxing sensation Tenshin Nasukawa, who he dropped three times and stopped inside one round - leaving the youngster in tears.

Following two more exhibition bouts, Mayweather returned to Japan to face MMA fighter Mikuru Asakura and survived an early flurry to finish the fight in the second stanza.

It’s expected he will return for another fight with RIZIN, who could be adding another big name to their roster if Diaz’s recent social media activity is anything to go by.
