Overall Meaning

In Shangela's song Call me LaQuifa, the artist is adopting the persona of LaQuifa, a postmodern drag queen who, according to the lyrics, is not to be underestimated. LaQuifa is sassy, assertive and not afraid to demand attention or respect. In the verses, Shangela switches to her own persona, Nancy Drew, implying that she too is a sharp, no-nonsense character who is on a mission to get to the truth. Shangela represents herself as a strong, determined woman, who is not to be messed with.


The repetition of the phrase “I said what?” in the chorus emphasizes the power of the song's message. This phrase is used as a challenge to anyone who doubts LaQuifa's or Nancy Drew's authority. The chorus also features a call-and-response between the artist and the audience, which encourages participation and further emphasizes the message of assertiveness.


The final verse is a motivational message about overcoming obstacles and persevering. Shangela encourages the listener to pick themselves up and carry on, working the “Hallelu”. This provides both inspiration and empowerment and aligns with the rest of the song's themes of strength, power, and determination.
