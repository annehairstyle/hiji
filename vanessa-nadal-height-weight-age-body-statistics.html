Vanessa Nadal is an American scientist, lawyer, and lecturer who began working as an adjunct professor at her alma mater, Fordham University School of Law, in 2019. There, she became well recognized for her work as the co-creator and co-professor of the first course on U.S. Cosmetics Regulation which covers the governmental, industrial, and societal regulations on how to make and sell cosmetics. Her lectures at the school have covered topics such as misbranding in addition to the history of cosmetics laws.

Education

Vanessa had attended the Hunter College High School, a secondary school in the Carnegie Hill neighborhood on the Upper East Side of Manhattan. After her high school graduation, she joined the renowned Massachusetts Institute of Technology, a private land-grant research university in Cambridge, Massachusetts.

She had graduated from there in 2004 with a B.S. (Bachelor of Science) degree in chemical engineering. In 2007, she had enrolled herself in the Fordham University School of Law, the law school of Fordham University, a private Jesuit research university in New York City. She had graduated from there in 2010 with a J.D. (Juris Doctor) degree.

    Vanessa Nadal Facts

  • After she graduated from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 2004, Vanessa started working as a research and development scientist at Johnson & Johnson Consumer products company to develop innovative technologies for skincare products.
  • In 2007, intending to affect positive change in society, she joined Fordham University to study law. In 2010, after graduating from there, she had worked as a litigation associate at Jones Day, an American international law firm headquartered in Cleveland, Ohio. There, she worked on federal, state, and international disputes until May 2016.
  • Between June 2016 and December 2018, she worked independently as a legal consultant. During this time, she had provided legal protection counsel to the Climate Science Legal Defense Fund, an organization founded, in September 2011, by members of the climate science community to defend scientists under attack.
  • In 2017, she was supposed to have a cameo role on the popular sitcom Curb Your Enthusiasm. American comedian, writer, actor, director, and TV producer Larry David, the creator, and star of the sitcom was supposed to fall asleep on Vanessa’s shoulder, in that scene, while watching Hamilton, the popular musical that her husband Lin-Manuel Miranda is best known for. However, she became nervous when the scene was about to be filmed and the actress, producer, and director America Ferrera had to fill in for her.
