Rocío Dúrcal was a Spanish singer and actress who lived from 4 October 1944 to 25 March 2006. She became extremely popular in Mexico, earning the nickname Reina de las Rancheras (“Queen of Rancheras”).

Awarded by the Recording Latin Academy’s Governing Board honors musicians who have made outstanding artistic contributions throughout their careers, the Latin Grammy Award for musical excellence was given to Rocío Dúrcal in 2005.

Her debut feature was Luis Lucia’s 1962 film Canción de Juventud. The movie’s plot included a teen with a distinct personality. The film enjoyed enormous success with both audiences and reviewers.

Other Spanish-speaking nations where the film was screened also had the same success.

Rocío Dúrcal immediately rose to the top of Roco de La Mancha. She subsequently signed her first record deal with international Phonogram (now Universal Music).
