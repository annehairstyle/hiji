Liev Schreiber on his partner Naomi Watts: To me, were married (update)

Posted by Artie Phelan on Monday, June 17, 2024

Liev Schreiber covers the upcoming issue of Cigar Aficionado Magazine. I remember reading an excellent interview they did with Matthew McConaughey a while back, and they recently got The Dude Jeff Bridges to talk about his past pot use. They have a way of getting celebrities to open up, probably with some nice scotch and Cuban cigars.

Schreiber is really killing it on Showtime’s new series Ray Donovan, which wrapped its first season last month. You can watch the entire season on demand if you subscribe to Showtime, and I would highly recommend it.

Schreiber talks a little in this interview about his character’s cigar smoking, which I guess they have to fit in there. He also gets candid about his relationship with his longterm partner, Naomi Watts, and says that he considers himself married. Liev and Naomi have been together eight years and they have two children together, sons Sasha, 6, and Samuel, nearly 5. We’ve heard engagement rumors about these two for years, but they’ve never been officially engaged as far as I can tell. What I want to know is: does Naomi feel the same, like they’re married? Here’s more.

When Liev Schreiber is asked what traits he shares with Ray Donovan, the eponymous character of his critically lauded Showtime series, his response is immediate and definitive: “Not one blood cell. I love Ray, but I wouldn’t want to be him for very long.” The self-proclaimed “uncomfortable and awkward kid” and New York theater mainstay is featured on the cover of the November/December 2013 issue of Cigar Aficionado, on newsstands November 5.

Hitting it Hard examines Schreiber’s relationship with longtime partner Naomi Watts (“To me, we’re married, though I guess that, legally, we’re a domestic partnership. I’m just happy we’re a great couple.”), as well as his oft-hidden comedic side (filmmaker Greg Mottola says, “People don’t know how funny he is. He loves a good prank or juvenile joke”). Schreiber has used cigars as a way into characters, both onstage and on film: “There’s something decadent that smoking a cigar passes on to a character, something relaxed and confident –or arrogant and brash.” He admits, “I really enjoy that feeling of being inebriated with the smoke. I only make it through about 10 puffs because I can’t resist inhaling.”

[From Cigar Aficionado, received via email]

That’s sweet what he said about Naomi. He sounds like he thinks of himself as a team with her, and that’s how it should be. Have you seen these photos of Naomi, Liev and their kids on their bikes and scooter riding around Manhattan? In one set of photos, Naomi is riding behind Liev on the same bike as their two kids. That looks dangerous and Naomi looks like she’s grimacing! They need a tandem bike if they all want to pile on like that. I love them though.
