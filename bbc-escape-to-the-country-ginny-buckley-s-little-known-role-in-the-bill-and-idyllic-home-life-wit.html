BBC Escape to the Country: Ginny Buckleys little known role in The Bill and idyllic home life wit

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Tuesday, June 11, 2024

TV presenter and journalist Ginny Buckley, 51, is perhaps best known for presenting BBC’s Escape to the Country.

Ginny has appeared on the show, along with fellow presenters Nicki Chapman and Jonnie Irwin, since 2015.

When she’s not helping hopeful property-buyers find their dream homes, Ginny puts her motoring expertise to good use as a journalist, and she even was announced as Good Housekeeping’s Motoring Editor in 2015.

Although Ginny is a familiar face on our screens, she has kept a number of details about her personal and private life away from public view.

For example, it isn’t clear whether or not Ginny is married, although some reports state she wears a wedding ring which would suggest she is.

What we do know from Ginny’s Instagram is that she is a mum to son Zak who turned 12 in August.

On a Zak’s birthday, Ginny opened up on social media about her love for him.

The TV presenter wrote: “And just like that my beautiful little boy is 12!

“Happy Birthday to my funny, kind and caring August boy, who is at his happiest down by the beach catching a wave!

“Built that ladder to the stars Zaka… I’m so proud to be your mum and I love you very much.”

It’s easy to forget that Ginny started her career in showbiz on the 90s children's game show The Wetter The Better.

She then went on to present as part of Granada Men and Motors in the early 2000s, and in 2010 she co-presented on BBC's Crimewatch with presenter Rav Wilding.

Something a number of viewers forget about Ginny is that she had a short stint as an actress - she reportedly played a newsreader in a 2008 episode of The Bill, which was also her TV debut.
