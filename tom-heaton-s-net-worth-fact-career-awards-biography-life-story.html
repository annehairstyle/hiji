Thomas David Heaton is an English professional football player who plays for Premier League club Aston Villa and the England national team as a goalkeeper. He started playing football for the youth team of Wrexham. In 2002, he moved to the youth team of Manchester United and began his career with Manchester United but was unable to break into the first team and spent time on loan with Swindon Town, Royal Antwerp, Cardiff City, Queens Park Ranger, Rochdale, and Wycombe Wanderers. After his release by Manchester United, on 1 July 2010, he joined Cardiff City permanently where he made 29 appearances for the club. In 2012, he then joined Bristol City on a one-year deal with an option for a further 12 months and made his debut for the club on 18 August 2012. In May 2013, he joined Burnley after turning down a further one-year deal at Bristol City. After playing six years at Burnley, on 1 August 2019, he signed for newly-promoted Premier League club Aston Villa for an undisclosed fee, believed to be £8 million. He made his debut for the club on 10 August 2019.

On the international level, he has represented England at various age levels. He has represented England at U16, U17, U18, U19, U21, and the senior level. He made his England national team debut on 27 May 2016 in a UEFA Euro 2016 warm-up fixture.

Tom Heaton Club Career

  • He started playing football for the youth team of Wrexham.

  • After returning from QPR, he joined Rochdale on 13 November 2009 until the end of December 2009. On 14 November 2009, he made his debut for the club in a 3-2 loss against Chesterfield. The loan was then extended until 23 January 2010 before a further extension pushed the end date back to 31 January. During his time at Rochdale, he played over 12 matches for the club where he conceded only 10 goals, including four clean sheets, and finished on the losing team just once.

  • After his release from Manchester United, on 1 July 2010, he joined Cardiff City permanently. During the 2010-11 season, with his performance, he earned him the club’s Young Player of the Year award. During his time at Cardiff City, he made 40 appearances for the club in all the competitions.
  • In 2012, he joined Bristol City on a one-year deal with an option for a further 12 months and made his debut for the club on 18 August 2012 in a 1-0 defeat away to Nottingham Forest. During his time at Bristol City, he started 43 of Bristol City’s 46 matches in the Championship that season and was viewed as one of the club’s best players that season.
  • In May 2013, he joined Burnley after turning down a further one-year deal at Bristol City. He established himself as a first-choice goalkeeper within a short period and made his debut for the team in a 1-1 home draw against Bolton Wanderers in August 2013. In his first season, he was a part of Burnley’s defence that allowed only 37 goals, the fewest in the league, as Burnley returned to the Premier League for the first time since 2009-10 where he also picked up 19 clean sheets, the most by any keeper in the regular season.
  • On 18 August 2014, he made his Premier League debut in a 3-1 loss against Chelsea. During the 2014-15 season, he made 40 appearances and kept 10 clean sheets in all the competitions as he was voted by his teammates as Burnley’s Players’ Player of the Year.
  • On 30 June 2015, he signed a new three-year deal at the club through to the summer of 2018. In the 2015-16 season, he was named as captain for that season and captained the team for the first time on 8 August 2015 in a 1-1 draw against Leeds United on the opening day of the 2015-16 Championship season. During that season, he made 48 appearances and kept 20 clean sheets in all the competitions as Burnley won the 2015-16 Football League Championship and earned promotion back to the Premier League at their first attempt. Towards the end of the season, he was named in the Championship PFA Team of the Year.
  • On 20 July 2016, he signed another new contract with the club until the summer of 2020. On 22 January 2017, he made his 150th league appearance for the club in a 2-1 loss against Arsenal. During the 2016-17 season, he made 36 appearances and kept 10 clean sheets in all the competitions.

  • During the 2018-19 season, he made 22 appearances and kept 4 clean sheets in all the competitions.
  • After playing six years at Burnley, on 1 August 2019, he signed for newly-promoted Premier League club Aston Villa for an undisclosed fee, believed to be £8 million. He made his debut for the club on 10 August 2019 in a 3-1 loss against Tottenham Hotspurs. On 1 January 2020, he suffered a season-ending knee injury during a 2-1 win against his former team Burnley. During the 2019-20 season, due to his injury, he was kept out of the field for the rest of the season where he made 20 appearances and kept 4 clean sheets in all the competitions.
