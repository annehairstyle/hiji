Olivia Cooke Bio (Wiki)

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Monday, June 17, 2024

Who is Olivia Cooke?

Olivia Cooke is an actress from the United Kingdom.

Olivia Cooke played Emma Decode in the A&E drama-thriller series Bates Motel (2013–2017) and Becky Sharp in the period drama miniseries Vanity Fair (2018).

Who are the parents of Olivia Cooke?

Olivia was born on December 27, 1993, in Oldham, England.

She is 28 years old as of 2023.

Her Full name is Olivia Kate Cooke.

When she was a child, her parents split, and she and her sister moved in with their mother.

Talking about her nationality, she holds a British identity.

After four years in New York City, she moved to London in January 2020.

Olivia Cooke starred as a student getting a piggyback ride from Harry Styles in One Direction’s “Autumn Term” tour video in 2012.

Despite her agent’s advice that she should not go to drama school because she was already performing, she applied to the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art (RADA) and made it to the final round of auditions, but was not accepted.

Education

She began performing at the Oldham Theatre Workshop, an after-school drama program in her hometown when she was eight years old.

She went to Royton and Crompton Academy and Oldham Sixth Form College to study theater before quitting before her A-levels to star in the drama series Blackout.

Olivia Cooke- Professional Life, Careers

Olivia is an English actress who has also appeared in the horror films Ouija (2014), Me and Earl and the Dying Girl (2015), The Limehouse Golem (2016), Thoroughbreds (2017), Ready Player One (2018), and Sound of Metal (2020).

She played Maria in a collegiate production of West Side Story before landing her first and only main part in Prom: The Musical, a version of Cinderella, for the Oldham Theatre Workshop.

Following her performance at the Oldham Theatre Workshop, Cooke was cast in television roles by Beverley Keogh, a casting director who worked next door to her agency.

Cooke played a young teacher in a dictatorial orphanage in the 1940s in all three BBC mini-series productions in 2012: Blackout as the daughter of Christopher Eccleston’s character, and The Secret of Crickley Hall as a young teacher at a despotic orphanage in the 1940s.

Despite her inexperience, Cooke stood out among European actors during the arduous casting process for The Quiet Ones, which premiered in April 2014, two years after production.

Following the release of The Quiet Ones in 2012, Cooke signed with a Los Angeles-based agent.

The Signal, Cooke’s second feature film, premiered at the 2014 Sundance Film Festival, starring Brenton Thwaites and Laurence Fishburne. Cooke then starred in Me and Earl and the Dying Girl, a comedy-drama.

The film received both the Grand Jury and Audience Awards at the Sundance Film Festival, where it premiered.

In 2015, Olivia Cooke co-wrote an episode of Axe Cop with her Me and Earl and the Dying Girl co-star Nick Offerman, in which she voiced the Loch Ness Monster.

In Steven Spielberg‘s science fiction adventure Ready Player One, Cooke played Art3mis, and the film was released in March 2018. Later the same year, she co-starred in Dan Fogelman‘s love drama Life Itself with Oscar Isaac, Olivia Wilde, and Samuel L. Jackson, and she played Becky Sharp in the ITV version of Vanity Fair.

Cooke starred in the drama film Sound of Metal, alongside Riz Ahmed.

The film premiered at the 2019 Toronto International Film Festival on 6 September 2019.
