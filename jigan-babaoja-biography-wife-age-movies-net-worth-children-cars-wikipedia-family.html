Biography

Kazeem Abimbola Ibrahim (born June 16, 1982), widely recognized as Jigan BabaOja, is a prominent Nigerian actor, comedian, and content creator renowned for his hilarious comedy skits shared across various online platforms.

Moreover, he played a pivotal role in popularizing the catchphrase “sho mo age mi,” which originated from one of his comedic skits, “Jigan Baba Oja.” Beyond his comedic talents, Jigan BabaOja is also a proficient actor in the Nollywood industry, earning acclaim for his performances in Yoruba films.

Early Life & Education

Born in Bariga, Lagos State on June 16, 1982, Jigan BabaOja emerged as a gifted and humorous comedian. Raised under the loving care of his parents, his father, Chief Mudashiru Kazeem, played a significant role in his upbringing, while details about his mother remain undisclosed. Unfortunately, there’s little information available about Jigan Babaoja‘s siblings.

For his education, Jigan BabaOja attended Odunlade Basic School in Malu, Somalia, before moving on to Moroccan Senior School in Yaba, where he completed his basic and senior education. Subsequently, he pursued higher studies, earning a bachelor’s degree in business administration from Olabisi Onabanjo University.

Career

In 2001, Jigan BabaOja stepped into the music scene, showcasing his passion and talent through a series of beautiful songs and visually stunning music videos.

During this period, he co-founded the group “BBK,” where they showcased their musical prowess to the world. Sadly, their promising collaboration ended due to financial disputes, leading to their disbandment. However, Jigan‘s resilience and unwavering love for music kept him pushing forward.

In 2008, Jigan BabaOja embarked on a solo career and teamed up with the renowned Fuji artist Pasuma to release a single. Alongside his musical pursuits, Jigan explored acting, drawing inspiration from his life experiences to create the movie Disable. 

His acting journey gained momentum with roles in Mr. Kola, directed by Kunle Afod and Funsho Adeolu. Yet, his comedic sketches, notably the viral “Sho Mo Age Mi,” cemented Jigan‘s status as a versatile and talented actor.
