Marlo Thomas

Posted by Billy Koelling on Tuesday, June 25, 2024

Marlo Thomas is an actress, producer, author, and social activist from the United States. She is best known for her role in the iconic sitcom “That Girl.” Thomas is known for her role in the award-winning children’s franchise “Free to Be… You and Me” as well as her children’s album “Marlo Thomas and Friends: Thanks & Giving All Year Long,” for which she won a Grammy Award.

She has received four Emmys, a Golden Globe, a Peabody Award, and a Grammy Award over the course of her four-decade career, as well as the “Presidential Medal of Freedom” in 2014. In addition, Thomas is a member of the Broadcasting and Cable Hall of Fame. Thomas is also active on social media, with accounts such as @marlothomas on Instagram and @MarloThomas on Twitter.

Where was Marlo Thomas Born?

Marlo Thomas was born in the United States on November 21, 1937, in Detroit, Michigan. Margaret Julia Thomas is her given name. Her country of origin is the United States of America. Thomas is of White descent, and her zodiac sign is Scorpio.

As the eldest child of Roman Catholic Lebanese American comedian Danny Thomas (1912 – 1991) and his wife, the former Sicilian American Rose Marie Cassaniti (1914 – 2000), Marlo grew up in a well-to-do household. Danny, her father, was a well-known comedian, musician, actor, producer, and philanthropist who received numerous awards over the course of his five-decade career, including a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.

She grew up in Beverly Hills, California, with her two younger siblings: Terre, her sister, and Tony Thomas, her brother, who is now a television and film director.

Marlo attended Los Angeles’ Marymount High School before enrolling at the University of Southern California and receiving a teaching degree.

She also studied drama at the HB Studio in New York City’s Greenwich Village. Thomas also belonged to the Kappa Alpha Theta sorority. She started her career as a schoolteacher before moving on to become an actress.
