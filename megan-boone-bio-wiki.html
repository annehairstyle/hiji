Megan Boone Bio (Wiki)

Posted by Kelle Repass on Tuesday, June 25, 2024

Who is Megan Boone?

Megan Boone is an American actress who is immensely famous for her work as an actress in the NBC drama series ‘The Blacklist‘ portraying the character Elizabeth Keen, an FBI agent.

She also made herself appear in some of the films and television shows ‘Law & Order: LA’, ‘My Bloody Valentine 3D’, ‘Step Up Revolution’, etc.

Megan Boone: Age, Parents, Ethnicity

Megan Boone was born to American parents in Petoskey, Michigan, the United States of America on April 29, 1983.

Her nationality is American and her ethnicity is mixed(German, Italian, Scottish, Dutch).

Her mother’s name is Jennifer Parr and her father’s name is Marcus Whitney Boone. Mrs. Jennifer is the Director of Sales.

Her birth name of Megan Whitney Boone. She was raised in The Villages, Florida, United States, with her grandparents.

Her grandfather, H. Gary Morse was a billionaire.

As a child, she was closer to her grandparents, the developer of ‘The villages‘.

Megan Boone has a sister named Paige Boone and a brother named Tom Boone.

In which school did Megan Boone attend?

So, to make her dream come true, she studied Bachelor of Fine Arts (BFA) in Acting at Florida State University’s School of Theatre.

Before attending  Florida State University’s School of Theatre, she was a student at Belleview High School and graduated in 2001.

She graduated from Florida State University’s School of Theater in 2005 and was exposed to practical learning with  Jane Alexander and Edwin Sherin at the Asolo Repertory Theatre.

Thus, she had a strong base for her acting career to be one of the greatest achievements in her life.

Megan Boone: Professional Life, Career

Before playing the debut role in the feature horror film ‘My Bloody Valentine 3D‘  in 2009, Megan Boone appeared in ‘Limonade Tous Les Jours’ and won  ‘LA Weekly Theater Awards.

Megan Boone also played in ‘Sex and the City 2’ and appeared regularly on the short-lived NBC series ‘Law & Order: LA’ as the role of Attorney Lauren Stanton.

In 2012, she won the ‘Gen Art Film Festival Award for Best Actress for her drama ‘Leave Me As You Found Me’ which was followed by her breakout role as FBI agent Elizabeth Keen in the NBC series ‘The Blacklist’.

In ‘The Blacklist’, she was the first cast member. Apart from acting, Megan directed wrote, and produced a 2010 short film titled, Eggshells for Soil.
