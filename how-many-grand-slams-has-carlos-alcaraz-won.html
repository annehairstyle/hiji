How many Grand Slams has Carlos Alcaraz won?

Posted by Artie Phelan on Tuesday, June 25, 2024

Spanish tennis sensation Carlos Alcaraz is deemed to be the next big thing in the world of tennis. Alcaraz is currently ranked World No. 2, as of December 19, 2023. With his exceptional performance on the court, he has won twelve singles titles in his professional career. Alcaraz became the youngest man in the Open Era to top the singles ranking following his victory at the 2022 US Open. He is a right-handed player who turned pro in 2018.

Alcaraz ended his season by reaching the semifinals of the 2023 ATP Finals, where World Champion Novak Djokovic defeated him. He played his longest-ever best-of-three-sets ATP Masters final at the 2023 Cincinnati Final. He lost to Djokovic despite having a championship point in the second-set tiebreak. He won his first title on grass at the 2023 Queen Club Championships by defeating Alex de Minaur in the final. Alcaraz prefers to play an all-court game by relying on his forehand short.

How many titles does Carlos Alcaraz have in all Grand Slams?

Carlos Alcaraz has won two grand slam titles, as of December 2023. He won his first Grand Slam title at the 2022 US Open, defeating Norway's Casper Ruud with a score of 6–4, 2–6, 7–6, 6–3. His second grand slam victory came via a win over Novak Djokovic in the 2023 Wimbledon Championship. He defeated Djokovic in a 5-set thriller to win his first Wimbledon title.

When did Carlos Alcaraz win his first Grand Slam title?

Carlos Alcaraz won his first Grand Slam title on September 11, 2022, at the US Open by defeating Casper Ruud 6-4, 2-6, 7-6, 6-3 in the finale. With the victory, he took home his first major championship.

For the top spot, Ruud, Rafael Nadal, Daniil Medvedev, and Stefanos Tsitsipas were also contenders. In the championship match against Jannik Sinner in the quarterfinals, Alcaraz saved a match point to win.

How many Grand Slam titles does Carlos Alcaraz have in singles?

Carlos Alcaraz has two grand slam titles in singles including the 2023 Wimbledon Open and the 2022 US Open. He won the US Open 2022 at the age of 19 years, 4 months, and 6 days. Alcaraz has won four Masters 1000 titles, the US Open in 2022, and the Wimbledon Championship in 2023.
