Where was Renée Felice Smith born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Renée and her family spent the majority of her childhood in Patchogue, New York, where she attended Patchogue-Medford High School before enrolling at New York University’s Tisch School of Arts, where she graduated with a double major in journalism and a minor in history.

She also attended the Lee Strasberg Institute in New York City. Her childhood friends nicknamed her ‘Ray Ray,’ and she is known to enjoy dancing in her spare time. There isn’t much information about her family size, so she may have siblings.

While the exact date of Renée’s wedding is unknown, she has been married to her husband, American filmmaker Christopher Gabriel, for nearly a decade. They are more than just a couple; Renée and Christopher, for example, co-directed the film “The Relationtrip.”

Her Instagram feed is filled with pictures of the couple, leading one to believe that their relationship is as strong as ever, even though no plans for children have been announced. They’re also a popular couple at events, so they rarely spend time apart in public.

How did Renée Felice Smith start her Professional Career?

She was six years old when she starred in a Dannon yogurt commercial, which catapulted her to national prominence. Her first significant role was as Astor in “Wildflower,” an off-Broadway play at Manhattan’s Second Stage’s McGinn-Cazale Theatre. She then landed her first TV series role as Dinah Thorpe in “The Wyoming Story” in 2010, followed by her first TV film role as Dinah Thorpe in “Viralcom” in 2008.

She starred as Emma in Tony Kaye’s “Detachment,” alongside Adrien Brody, a year later. Following minor roles in films such as “That Thing with the Cat,” “Nanny Cam,” “The Relationtrip,” and “That Thing with the Cat! “, she went on to star in her own feature film, “That Thing with the Cat! ”

Historically, she was finally cast in the show that would catapult her to fame. Renée rose to prominence as Nell Jones on “NCIS: Los Angeles” in 2010. As long as the show is on the air, it remains to be seen where her newfound fame will take her next.
