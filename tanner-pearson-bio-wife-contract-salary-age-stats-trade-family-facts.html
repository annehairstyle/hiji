Tanner Pearson Married Life: Wife & Baby

Tanner Pearson is a married man with a wife of three years. The ice hockey player for the Vancouver Canucks walked down the aisle together with his long-haul girlfriend, Meaghan Loveday, in the summer of 2018.


Pearson and his wife Meaghan are long-time love, who started dating in 2010. They got engaged in the summer of 2017 after dating for seven years.

Meaghan, born in 1992, also hails from Kitchener, Ontario. She is the daughter of Tricia Loveday, who serves as an executive assistant at a company named Manulife.

Loveday attended the University of Waterloo in Waterloo, Canada. There, the 6-foot played college volleyball for the Waterloo Warriors. She holds an arts degree from the University of Waterloo.

After sharing the wedding rings on 14 August 2018, Tanner welcomed a beautiful son named Tucker Pearson with his wife. The pair was blessed with their first child on 19 May 2019. Tanner frequently posts pictures of his son and his wife on his Instagram account.

Tanner Pearson Bio & Family: Age, Parents, Height

The Canadian professional ice hockey player Tanner Pearson was born in 1992 in Kitchener, Ontario. He is 28-years-of-age as of now and celebrates his birthday on 10th August every year. Tanner is Canadian by nationality and his ethnicity is white.

Talking about his family life, Tanner is among the two children of his parents, Kim Pearson and Tim Pearson. He shares a sibling bond with his sister named Ali Pearson.

Tanner’s father, Tim, and his elder sister, Ali, both were involved in hockey. His father served as an executive in the equipment company named Bauer Hockey. Moreover, he has been working in the hockey business for three decades.


Similarly, his sister worked for the major junior ice hockey team, Kitchener Rangers. As of now, Ali works as a Commercial Underwriter at Wawanesa Insurance.

Tanner stands tall at the height of 6 feet and 1 inch (1.85 meters), and he weighs around 198 pounds (90 kg).

His NHL Career: Trade, Pittsburgh Penguins, Stats

Before joining the National Hockey League, Tanner played major junior hockey with the Barrie Colts in the Ontario Hockey League. He was then drafted as the 30th overall pick by the Los Angeles Kings in the 2012 NHL Entry Draft.

Tanner ringed his NHL debut on 18 May 2013 after starting as the right-winger in the Kings’ playoff bout against the San Jose Sharks. Later on 13 June 2014, he helped his side to a Stanley Cup victory over the New York Rangers.

Tanner was part of the Kings team till the 2018-19 season, where he struggled by registering just one assist through 17 games.

As a result, he was traded to the Pittsburgh Penguins on 14 November 2018. Just more than three months later, Tanner moved to Vancouver Canucks with a trade on 25 February 2019.

From his 457 regular season starts for three different NHL teams, Tanner records the stats of 108 goals and 107 assists as of February 2021.

His Contract & Salary

The ice hockey player first penned a three-year entry-level contract with the Los Angeles Kings on 3 August 2012. Following that, he agreed to a two-year contract extension worth $2.8 million on 2 April 2015.

Later in the 2017-18 regular season, Tanner agreed to another contract extension, this time for four years worth $15 million on 8 May 2017. As per the terms of his contract, he earns an average annual salary of $3,750,000.

Some Interesting Facts About Tanner Pearson

  • Tanner won a bronze medal representing Canada at the 2012 World Junior Ice Hockey Championship.
  • His career awards and honors include Subway Super Series – Team OHL (2011) and Second Team All-Star (2011-12).
  • Tanner completed his first professional season with the Kings’ affiliate, the Manchester Monarchs of the American Hockey League.
  • His sister Ali graduated from Conestoga College with a Business Administration and Marketing degree in 2008.
